Garden Game of Life

Background

The universe of the Game of Life is an infinite, two-dimensional orthogonal grid of square cells, each of which is in one of two possible states, live or dead (or populated and unpopulated, respectively).

Updated Rules

In Garden of Life, we've modified the rules to the following:
1. Any live plant with fewer than two live neighbours dies, as if by underpopulation.
2. Any live plant with two or three live neighbours lives on to the next generation.
3. Any live plant with more than three live neighbours dies, as if by overpopulation.
4. Any dead plant with exactly three live neighbours becomes a sprout, as if by reproduction.
5. Any sprout with exactly three live neighbours becomes a flower, as if by reproduction.
